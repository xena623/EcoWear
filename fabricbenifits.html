    • Sustainable Local Fabrics

    Organic Cotton

    Details: Grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers, organic cotton farming promotes healthier soil and water systems.
    It avoids the harmful chemicals found in conventional cotton farming.
    Benifits:

    • Uses less water than conventional cotton
    • Safer for the eco system and farmers
    • Soft to the skin, great for allergies
    • Biodegradable and compostable at the end of its life.

    Hemp

    Details: Hemp is a highly sustainable crop that grows quickly with minimal water and no need for pesticides.
    The fibers are strong and durable, often blended with other materials for clothing.
    Benifits:

    • Requires little to no water and chemicals to produce
    • Naturally pest-resistant and grows densely, reducing land use when grown.
    • Breathable and antimicrobial properties.
    • Becomes softer and more comfortable the more you wear it.

    Linen (Flax)

    Details: Made from flax plants, linen uses less water and pesticides than cotton. It’s a traditional fabric known for its cooling properties,
    perfect for warm climates.
    Benifits:

    • Strong, durable and long lasting.
    • Breathable and moisture-wicking, keeping skin cool.
    • Naturally biodegradable and easily compostable.
    • Requires less materials to produce compared to conventional cotton

    Bamboo (Mechanical Processed)

    Details: Bamboo can be turned into fabric mechanically (like linen), avoiding harsh chemicals.
    This fabric is soft and breathable, but beware chemically processed bamboo (viscose), which is less eco-friendly unless done responsibly.
    Benifits:

    • Fast-growing crop with minimal water and no pesticides.
    • Naturally antibacterial and hypoallergenic.
    • Soft and silky texture.
    • Biodegradable if processed without chemicals.

    Tencel (Lyocell)

    Details:Tencel is made from sustainably harvested eucalyptus or other wood pulp using a closed-loop system that recycles water and solvents.
    It’s one of the most eco-friendly semi-synthetic fibers.
    Benifits:

    • Soft, breathable, and moisture-wicking.
    • Uses less water and energy than cotton.
    • Closed-loop production reduces chemical waste.
    • Biodegradable and compostable.

    Recycled Polyester

    Details: Made by melting and reforming plastic waste like PET bottles, recycled polyester saves plastic from landfills and oceans.
    Though synthetic, it reduces reliance on fossil fuels compared to virgin polyester.

    • Reduces plastic pollution by reusing waste.
    • Durable and moisture-wicking, great for activewear.
    • Uses less energy and resources than new polyester.
    • Often blended with natural fibers for comfort.

    Wool (Sustainably Sourced)

    Details:Sourced from sheep raised with animal welfare in mind, sustainable wool is renewable and biodegradable.
    Wool production focuses on ethical farming and land management.

    • Naturally insulating and moisture-wicking.
    • Renewable resource—sheep grow new wool every year.
    • Biodegradable, breaks down naturally in soil.
    • Durable and long-lasting, reducing clothing turnover.
